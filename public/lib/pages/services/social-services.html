Social Services

Tangentyere’s Social Services Department aims to improve the quality of life of Town Camp residents and the wider community, through providing innovative, responsive and culturally appropriate services.

Our dedicated team of primarily Aborginal Staff work in close partnership with government and other non-government agencies to deliver these services as well as advocate for the rights of our members.

Social Justice Programs

Aborginal Community Patrols & Community Safety

Tangentyere Patrols are prevention and acute intervention services, staffed by Aboriginal people who work to resolve disputes and intervene in situations of family conflict before harm is caused.

Tangentyere Night Patrol has been operating since 1990, with Community Day Patrol and Youth Night Patrol coming under Tangentyere’s Social Justice Programs in 2007.

Er and ID Card Service

Overview

The ER and ID Card Service can provide support to people who are:

  • Sleeping Rough
  • Homeless
  • 'At Risk' of Homelessness
  • Escaping Violence
  • Remote Community Visitors
  • Released Prisoners

The ER and ID Card Service can provide the following categories of support:

  • Proof of Identity Card
    • Emergency Relief
  • Case Management
  • Referrals

Tangentyere ID Card

The Tangentyere Card is photo ID which is accepted by banks and service providers.

To obtain a Tangentyere Card a client must be able to provide at least 1 document from column 1 and 1 document from column 2:
